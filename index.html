Ankush Gola

I'm a software engineer at Facebook NY. Recently, I graduated from Princeton University with a degree in electrical engineering. I enjoy working on interesting hardware and software projects.

Projects

Here are some of the things I've worked on. Many of these are side projects that were built within a 48-hour timeframe during various hackathons. Hackathons have provided a great environment to turn lingering ideas into working prototypes. I have tried (and will try) to participate in as many as possible. Click on the title or image to learn more about each project.

Dubstep Laser Harp

A sound synthesizer with lasers as the human interface. Plays dubstep at various frequencies based on which laser beam is hit and hand position. Built in 48 hours in a team of four.
First Place, HackPrinceton Fall 2012

Robot Kinection

A rover-like robot that is controlled using hand gestures read by a Microsoft Kinect. Built in 48 hours in a team of four.
First Place, HackPrinceton Spring 2013

Pi-Angulate

A Raspberry Pi based sound trilateration system that dynamically approximates the location of a given sound source. Built in 24 hours in a team of four.
Finalist, Greylock Hackfest Summer 2013

Bernice

A vehicle built on an RC-chassis that is controlled by a virtual reality station. Built over the course of of 6 weeks in a team of two.
Final Project, ELE 302, Spring 2014

Histograph

A webapp that analyzes a user's browsing habits and displays patterns with intuitive graphics. Recommendations for other websites are generated using collaborative filtering. Not creepy at all.
Final Project, COS 333, Spring 2014

WhenDo.io

A plug-and-play system for Arduino equipped with a board to auto-detect sensors and configurations, as well as an intuitive visual programming language that compiles into C++. Built in 24 hours in a team of 4.
Second Place, Greylock Hackfest, Summer 2014

Byke

An automatic bike transmission system mounted on a bike that changes gears to maintain the biker's cadence. Built in 48 hours in a team of 3.
Second Place, HackPrinceton Fall 2014

IneFISHency

A rover-like robot with a fishtank mounted ontop. A camera detects the fish's movements and translates them into robot motion. Essentially a hamster ball for a goldfish. No fish were harmed.
First Place, HackPrinceton Spring 2015
